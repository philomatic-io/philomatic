Why folders never fixed this.

Three copies that will never hear about each other. You can’t see where the ideas connect — and what you learned last month can’t resurface to shape what you’re learning today. Folders don’t connect. Folders just make smaller piles — full of redundant ideas that are hard to find.

Concepts connect.

One idea, many journeys. Every new domain plugs into what you already know — the sources you read, the passages you clipped, the questions you answered all come with you. Learning compounds instead of starting over.

Your journey is someone’s head start.

Publish it as a track — the idea, the sources, the order — and it becomes a page anyone can follow. Someone forks it, bends it toward rye, and passes it on. The way you learned becomes something others build on.
